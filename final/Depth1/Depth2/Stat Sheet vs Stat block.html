Stat Sheet vs Stat Block

In Dungeons & Dragons, players and Dungeon Masters use two different tools to track information: stat sheets for player characters and stat blocks for monsters. They look similar at a glance, but they serve very different purposes.

What a Stat Sheet Is

A stat sheet is used by a player to track everything about their character. It includes ability scores, skills, equipment, spells, personality details, and anything else the character can do or carry.

  • Full character details
  • Used every session
  • Tracks long‑term growth and leveling
  • Shows what the character is capable of

What a Stat Block Is

A stat block is used by the Dungeon Master to run monsters and NPCs. It is short, simple, and designed for quick reference during encounters.

  • Only the information needed to run the creature
  • Fast to read during combat
  • Includes attacks, abilities, and defenses
  • Does not track long‑term progression

Why They’re Different

Players need a lot of detail because they control one character for the entire campaign. The DM, however, may run dozens of creatures in a single session, so stat blocks must be fast and simple.
