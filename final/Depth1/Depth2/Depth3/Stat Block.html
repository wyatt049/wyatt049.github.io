Stat Block

What Is a Stat Block?

A stat block is the mechanical summary of a monster. It shows everything the Dungeon Master needs to run that creature in combat or during an encounter. While a stat sheet describes a player character, a stat block describes a monster’s abilities, behavior, strengths, and weaknesses.

Stat blocks are designed to be fast to read and easy to use during gameplay. They focus on combat‑ready information rather than story or personality.

What’s Inside a Stat Block?

Below are the major parts of a typical monster stat block, along with a short explanation of what each one does.

Name and Type: The creature’s name and what kind of monster it is.

Armor Class: How hard the monster is to hit.

Hit Points: How much damage the monster can take before being defeated.

Speed: How far the monster can move on its turn.

Ability Scores: The monster’s Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma.

Saving Throws: Special defenses the monster is better at resisting.

Skills: Areas where the monster is especially talented.

Damage Resistances: Damage types the monster takes less damage from.

Damage Immunities: Damage types the monster cannot be harmed by.

Condition Immunities: Conditions the monster cannot suffer.

Senses: Special ways the monster perceives the world.

Languages: What the monster can speak or understand.

Challenge Rating (CR): A number showing how dangerous the monster is.

Actions

Actions are the things the monster can do on its turn. These include attacks, special moves, or magical abilities. Some monsters also have Multiattack, which lets them make several attacks at once.

Reactions

Some monsters can take special actions outside their turn when certain conditions are met. These are called reactions and often involve defensive abilities or counterattacks.

Special Traits

Many monsters have unique abilities that define how they behave in combat. These traits might give them resistances, special senses, movement abilities, or magical effects.
