Creature Types

What Are Creature Types?

Creature types are categories that describe what a monster is. These types help determine how spells, abilities, and effects interact with the creature. For example, some spells only affect humanoids, while others specifically target undead or fiends.

A creature’s type doesn’t describe its personality or behavior — it simply tells you what kind of being it is in the world of the game.

Aberration: Strange, alien creatures with unnatural biology or minds.

Beast: Ordinary animals or giant versions of them, like wolves or bears.

Celestial: Holy beings from upper planes, such as angels.

Construct: Artificial creatures like golems or animated objects.

Dragon: Powerful reptilian creatures with magical abilities, including true dragons and drakes.

Elemental: Beings made of pure elemental energy such as fire, water, air, or earth.

Fey: Magical creatures tied to nature and trickery, like sprites or dryads.

Fiend: Evil beings from lower planes, including demons and devils.

Giant: Massive humanoid creatures such as ogres, trolls, and true giants.

Humanoid: People and person‑like creatures, including humans, elves, dwarves, and goblins.

Monstrosity: Creatures that don’t fit other categories — often unnatural or magical beasts.

Ooze: Amorphous, blob‑like creatures such as slimes and jellies.

Plant: Living plant creatures like shambling mounds or awakened trees.

Undead: Creatures animated by necromantic energy, such as zombies, skeletons, and ghosts.
