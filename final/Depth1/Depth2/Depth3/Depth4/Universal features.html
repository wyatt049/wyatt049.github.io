Universal Features

What Are Universal Features?

Universal Features are the basic mechanical traits that every character has, no matter their class, race, or background.

These features define how tough the character is, how fast they move, what they can carry, and how they interact with the world in a general way.

These are the “core stats” that appear on every character sheet.

They don’t depend on special abilities or class choices — they’re simply part of being a creature in the game.

Hit Points (HP)

Hit Points represent how much damage a character can take before falling unconscious. Higher HP means the character can survive longer in combat. HP is based on the character’s class and Constitution score, and it increases as the character levels up.

Armor Class (AC)

Armor Class determines how hard the character is to hit. A higher AC means attacks are more likely to miss. AC can come from armor, shields, natural defenses, or agility, depending on the character’s build.

Speed / Movement

Speed shows how far the character can move on their turn, usually measured in feet. Most characters have a base speed of 30 feet, but some races or abilities can increase or decrease this number.

Proficiency Bonus

The proficiency bonus is added to rolls the character is trained in, such as certain weapons, skills, or tools. It increases as the character levels up and represents growing experience and mastery.

Saving Throws

Saving throws are rolls used to resist harmful effects like spells, traps, poison, or environmental hazards. Every character has saving throw modifiers based on their ability scores, and some classes are naturally better at certain saves.

Languages

Languages represent what the character can speak, read, or write. Most characters start with a few languages based on their race and background. Knowing additional languages can help with communication, puzzles, or interacting with certain creatures.

Equipment

Equipment includes the gear the character carries — weapons, armor, tools, supplies, and other items. Equipment affects combat, exploration, and role‑playing, and it can change over time as the character acquires new items.
