Backstory

Your character’s backstory explains who they were before the adventure began. It can include their childhood, important events, motivations, fears, or anything that shaped who they are today.

As a child, Rakarth lived with his parents in the tribe in which he grew up. This tribe was extremely isolationist, hostile to all outsiders. When Rakarth was about 3 years old (equivalent to a 10 year old human), the shaman of the tribes received a vison. That vision showed an adult Rakarth laying waste to the tribe, slaughtering every man, woman and child with no mercy or hesitation. In order to prevent this future from coming true, he led the tribe in a sacrifice of the young Rakarth. Held down upon a rock, it was his mother who was given the knife to end him. To his horror, she seemed to have no problem with ending her son, no matter how much he begged or pleaded. Right as she raised the knife, ready to plunge it between his eyes and into his brain, the ground shook as lighting ripped through the sky. The people holding Rakarth down staggered, allowing him to move enough to barely avoid the dagger as it descended, feeling its cold steel cut down his snout and barely avoiding his eye. The ground shook again, sending the people of the tribe to the ground as they lost their balance. Using the opportunity presented to him, Rakarth hopped off of the sacrificial stone and ran into the woods. Using the skills his father had taught him as a hunter, Rakarth used them instead to avoid the hunters as they used him as their prey. Eventually, by the light of the early morning, Rakarth emerged into a field, where he saw a city. Hungry and exhausted, Rakarth hoped to find something to eat or at least somewhere to sleep. Wandering into the streets and down an alley, Rakarth prepared himself to sleep on the cold stone cobbles when a man in a set of black armor approached him. He offered to bring Rakarth into his order of paladins, the Order of the Five-Headed Queen. Rakarth didn't care nor understand the talk of gods or duty, but that is not what the stranger offered. He offered strength. The opportunity to become strong enough so that he would never be hurt again. Strong enough so that no one would dare to even try to hurt him, strong enough to make his tribe, his mother pay for what they did. And so, for the next 14 years, Rakarth trained as a paladin of Tiamat, fighting and scheming against the other initiates as much as with the order's enemies. The order taught him one thing above all: strength is everything. The strong take from the weak and the weak serve the strong. If Rakarth wanted to never serve, never be taken from again, he would have to become the strongest. And so now, at the adult age of 17 (Adult for Dragonborn) Rakarth sets out from his order on a quest to prove his strength and earn a spot among the proven and recognized paladins of Tiamat. His journey towards strength is just beginning.

Does It Need to Be This Long?

No your backstory does NOT have to be long. Some players write a few sentences, others write several pages. What matters is that it helps you understand your character and gives the DM something to work with.

As long as it is feasible and makes sense for your world then you are good to go.
