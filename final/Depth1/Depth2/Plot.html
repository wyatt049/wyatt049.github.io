Plot

The plot is the main storyline of an adventure. It gives the players a direction, a problem to solve, and a world to react to. A good plot doesn’t control the players — it simply sets the stage for their choices to matter.

Basic Structure

  • Setup — Introduces the world, the situation, and the hook that draws players in.
  • Rising Action — Challenges, discoveries, and obstacles that build tension.
  • Climax — The major confrontation or turning point of the story.
  • Resolution — The aftermath and consequences of the players’ actions.

Why Plots Matter

A plot gives the DM something to present and the players something to respond to. It keeps the adventure focused while still allowing freedom and creativity.
